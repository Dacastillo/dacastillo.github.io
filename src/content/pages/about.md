## Biography

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in Computational Physics, specializing in multiscale modeling of materials, quantum computing applications for chemistry, and machine learning interatomic potentials. My research bridges the gap between quantum mechanics and macroscopic material properties, enabling the design of novel nanomaterials with tailored properties.

Born in Santiago, Chile, I have dedicated my career to advancing scientific knowledge through computational methods. My work spans from fundamental quantum simulations to practical applications in materials science and drug discovery.

## Research Philosophy

I believe in the power of computational methods to unlock understanding of complex physical systems. By combining rigorous theoretical foundations with cutting-edge numerical techniques, we can predict and design materials with unprecedented precision. My approach emphasizes:

- **Multiscale thinking**: Connecting phenomena across length and time scales
- **Open science**: Sharing code, data, and methodologies to accelerate discovery
- **Interdisciplinary collaboration**: Bridging physics, chemistry, computer science, and engineering
- **Reproducibility**: Ensuring computational results are transparent and verifiable
